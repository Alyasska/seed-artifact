The Forge

Field Manual — game systems & rules · v0.2

A festival-scale, real-world strategy game · design sample for SEED · Aliaskar Bekishev · alyasska.github.io · github.com/Alyasska/seed-artifact

Hundreds of players, two-to-four factions, one outdoor field, a single 90-minute session. Capture-the-Flag crossed with a real-time strategy game, run by a costumed Game Master with NFC totems, an app, and LED towers for an interface. You take ground by standing on it and tapping it — there are no eliminations. Every attendee plays the full ninety minutes. This manual is the ruleset the balance sandbox tunes; the numbers below are its defaults (control resolves per 10-second tick; the economy ticks once a minute).

The Field

The field is a graph of sectors, each a physical control point (an NFC totem). Natural features — a creek, a ridge, the reservoir — split it into bridge-gated halves: those crossings are impassable until an Engineer builds a bridge. On a real venue (Lake San Antonio, a Lightning-in-a-Bottle site) the sectors snap to dry ground and the water becomes the ravine.

Control & Capture

Each sector carries a control meter from −100 (one faction) to +100 (the other). Standing on a sector adds presence; net presence drives the meter, capped so a single deathball can't steamroll. The meter flips ownership at ±60 (hysteresis), and uncontested ground slowly drifts back to neutral — you cannot hold the whole map passively.

Rule · Presence by Pulse

Presence = distinct bodies tapped in the last 60 s (re-tap to hold). Net pressure = (attackers − defenders) × class weight × capRate 6, capped at ±5. A ~3-net edge takes a neutral sector in about a minute. Holding is an active job — patrol, don't camp.

Player Classes

A faction's composition is a deck-building choice — too many Anchors can't take ground; too many Runners can't hold it. Presence weight is read on attack / on defense.

Runner

scout · intel

Presence 0.5 atk / 0.5 def · Move fast (×1.6)

Reveals fog for HQ; carries intel chits at double speed; can plant a feint.

Anchor

hold ground

Presence 1.0 atk / 2.0 def · Move slow (×0.6)

Dig In: +50% defensive control on the held sector. The wall a faction is built around.

Engineer

bridges

Presence 1.0 / 1.0 · Move normal

Builds a bridge at a gate (kit + 120 Supply); can sabotage one enemy bridge for 3 min.

Quartermaster

+50% yield

Presence 1.0 / 1.0 · Move normal

+50% Supply on the sector they occupy; carries double supply tokens.

Herald

Presence 1.0 / 1.0 · Move normal

Opens a parley channel between HQs; Rally grants allies +1 presence here for 60 s.

The Economy

Held sectors pay Supply into one faction pool. A per-sector yield cap means stacking is wasted — spread out. The trailing faction captures harder (rubber-band), the brake on snowball.

Bridges · Intel · Siege

Bridges open a gate over the ravine and are shared — your attack route is also your exposure. Intel is a resource: the HQ map is fogged, Runners scout, recon reports and comms-jams buy or deny information; a faction can win blind by out-scouting. Siege is the endgame: a stronghold's Wall (100) is invulnerable until the Siege phase, then falls only to bodies + a Supply dump (battering) after the attacker holds the delay tower for 3 continuous minutes.

The GM paces the phases and injects events (a supply drop, a storm that closes a region), sells intel, and adjudicates parley — the live balance valve the sandbox tells you where to set.

Balance Levers — the Honesty Ledger

Ten dials tune the game. Each states what it buys and what it costs.

Capture rate

intentmake taking a sector a ~minute push. tradefast churns the map, slow lets first-movers lock it. failstoo fast → flicker; too slow → stalemate.

Comeback aid

intentkeep the last 30 minutes alive for the trailing side. tradefights snowball but softens the reward for early play. failstoo high → the lead is meaningless.

Battering rate

intentmake the siege endgame reachable — bodies and Supply. tradefast is decisive, slow makes sieges rare. failstoo slow → sieges never finish.

What the Sandbox Found

  • Self-play found an exploit the four heuristics didn't (~100% vs the pool) — a better playtester.
  • Terrain flips balance: the same ruleset goes aggressor-dominant (~75%) on real Lake San Antonio. Balance is per-venue.
  • Pure turtling is non-viable (it can't cross the ravine) — a design call, with the levers to change it.

End vision. The pre-flight for The Forge: tune economy, class weights, and phase timings per venue in sim → ship a near-balanced rulebook → spend the few expensive on-field pilots validating how real humans behave.
